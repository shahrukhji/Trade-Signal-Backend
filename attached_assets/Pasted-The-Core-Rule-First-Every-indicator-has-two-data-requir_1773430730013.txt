The Core Rule First
Every indicator has two data requirements: a minimum (to even produce a value) and a recommended warmup (to produce a reliable value). EMA-based indicators especially need extra history because each EMA value carries a fraction of all prior history — the more data fed in, the more accurate it becomes.
​

For a 10-day EMA, StockCharts uses at least 250 periods to get accuracy within a fraction of a penny. This principle applies to all EMA-derived indicators.

Practical Answer for Your Setup
3 days of data is NOT enough for daily charts. Here's what you actually need:

Intraday trading (5-min/15-min candles): 3 days ≈ 225–375 candles. This is sufficient for RSI, MACD, Stochastic, ATR, and Bollinger Bands. Still marginal for ADX and EMA200.

Daily chart trading: You need at least 1 year (250 trading days) to cover SMA200/EMA200. For ADX to be reliable, load 2 years (500 days).

For all EMA-based indicators: Most platforms (TradingView, Zerodha Kite) auto-load 1–5 years — just ensure you're not restricting the date range manually.

Golden Rule
Load the maximum available history your platform allows. The calculation engine will use all of it. More past data = more accurate current indicator values, especially for EMA, MACD, ADX, and RSI. Never manually limit your chart to just 3 days unless you're purely doing intraday VWAP or Pivot Point trades
